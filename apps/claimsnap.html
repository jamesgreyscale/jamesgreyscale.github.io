ClaimSnap

Document a crash, step by step.

In the minutes after a crash it's hard to think straight, and the evidence disappears fast. ClaimSnap walks you through documenting it, step by step, so you capture what matters and keep it organized. It's yours to keep, and yours to use however you decide — whether that's filing with your own insurance or, if you choose, talking to an attorney.

  • Safety first: ClaimSnap opens by reminding you to check for injuries and call 911 — it never gets between you and emergency care, the police, or your insurer
  • A guided photo checklist that explains why each shot helps: vehicle damage, the scene, the other driver's plate, license and insurance card, signals, skid marks, and road conditions
  • Photos saved with the time and place they were taken
  • One organized file per accident — add the police report or medical documents over the following days
  • Export your file or share it with your own insurer
  • Plain-English guidance on what to do next, including your state's general filing-deadline information (general information, not legal advice)

A free case check — informational, no pressure

Answer a few questions and ClaimSnap gives you an honest, plain-English read on whether your situation is the kind injury attorneys typically help with. It's information to help you decide — not a promise about any outcome.

An attorney consultation — only if you choose

If — and only if — you decide you'd like legal help, you can request a free, no-obligation consultation with one law firm that handles auto-accident cases in your state. Nothing about you is shared with anyone unless you ask for that and give your consent. You're never obligated to hire anyone.

Built to be honest with you

ClaimSnap is not a law firm and does not give legal advice. We do not recommend or rank attorneys — firms are matched only by your location and case type. Participating firms pay ClaimSnap a flat fee for consultation requests; that fee never depends on whether you hire the firm or on any outcome of your case. Your documentation stays on your device unless you choose to share it, and you can delete everything anytime.

ClaimSnap is not a law firm and nothing in the app is legal or medical advice. If anyone is injured, call 911.
